Daddy, I'm coming home...

"The law" has been enforced to engender fear to those going in the wrong, and thereby rewarding a heavy penalty to the victors for their clandestine mis-doings. The irony in India lies in the fact that fear lingers amongst the victims, awaiting the attack of the sadists, the rapists.

The early morning sun shines its luminous light onto my closed eyes, as I shrink my eyes to the sudden bright beam. I turn over and crawl to reach my phone. The clock on my phone reads 6.55am. Shoot! "Have to get ready" I mourn. As I indulge and gobble up my morning breakfast, my eyes hover over the newspaper lying on the dining table. As the fan flutters the front page, I read along the headlines 'Verdict in Delhi Gang Rape Case today'. I heave a deep sigh. The clock on the wall reads 7.35, "Hurry hurry hurry" my mind repeats. Do I ask Dad to drop me at my bus stop, or do I run for it? I go for the latter. Waving a frantic bye to Dad, I start off with a brisk jog, looking at my watch now and then.

As I walk, I feel someone looking at me. I look to my right. A man stands, staring stealthily and running his eyes from top to bottom. I give him a disgusted look and move faster ahead. I reach the main road and wait for the vehicles to cross by as I need to go to the other side of the road. I cross halfway and wait for the other side vehicles to clear up. Quite suddenly, a truck zooms so close. I hear the truck driver screaming "Want to come home?". I stare in disbelief. My office shuttle arrives and I seat myself only for the words 'Want to come home?' rewinding and playing in my head. I plug my headphones in my ears, grit my teeth, and shut my eyes to 'let it go'.

I reach office safe and start working, a great distraction from my experience this morning, I strongly felt. My watch reads 11 and I walk to the pantry to get a nice steaming cup of tea. And so, my discussion with my colleagues begins at office.

"Dude, I had a crazy night partying yesterday! And the night was crazy only because of one dumb girl!" taunts my friend.

"What?" I say in pure disgust.

"Yeah dude. I'm sorry to say, but this is exactly why I feel girls get raped!"

"What??" I repeat, even more disgusted.

"Yeah. She was quite drunk and she was asking everyone at the night club to give her a lift."

"So? What does that got to do with girls getting raped?"

"No. I'm just saying this could be one of the many possible reasons for girls getting raped."

I just grit my teeth and walk away to my desk, to distract myself again. Women behave in a certain way, so they could get raped! Wow! Women should stay like statues, and even then they'd get raped... I wanted to scream! How can anyone give a reason to why women are being raped, apart from the fact that the rapists are purely horrifying, indecent, uncouth maniacs!

My phone rings and my dad calls. "Are you coming back home early?”, he asks. "No dad, I'm taking the 7.45 shuttle back home, I'll reach home only by 9.”, I reply.

"Alright, let me know when you reach your stop, I'll come to pick you up", he says. "Sure dad", I say and hang up.

As I take my shuttle back home, the day's events run in my head. I realise I've a certain amount of fear hitting me all the time, of what men could do if I walk alone on an empty street. The karate ought to me keep me safe. But why can't I learn karate because I want to learn karate, and not because my anxiety is taking a toll on me? The pepper spray, I got to keep it to spray the pepper particles into the rapists' eyes. What if I'm caught unawares? Then how can I use my weapon and protect myself? Protecting myself, that seems to be the only sensible solution for now, or is it? Oh god, I just want to get back home, I only want to get back home.

My shuttle stops and I get down. My dad waves and smiles. I hold his hand and we walk back home. I feel safe, daddy, I'm coming home.

- Priyanka Sridhar
